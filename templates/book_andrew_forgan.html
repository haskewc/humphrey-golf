The First Ever Golf Ball Collector?

Golfing Magazine in October 1907 ran an article on Andrew Forgan, the younger brother of Robert Forgan, highlighting his collection of 180 vintage golf balls.

"There is the 'Inglis Unique' named after Lord President Inglis, marked with a series of circles. There is the 'Mother' which no ball has ever more curiously been christened. This title has a very graceful history. The mould in which the ball was made was presented to Mr. Forgan by his mother when she paid him a visit at Perth in 1878; and in those days Â£5 was the price of a mould. It will be readily understood why Mr. Forgan called his new possession 'Mother's Mould'."

Andrew Forgan's collection represents one of the earliest documented golf ball collections, showcasing the diversity and craftsmanship of gutta-percha balls from the formative years of the game.

The Forgan Family Legacy

The Forgan name is synonymous with quality golf equipment. Robert Forgan established one of the most respected club-making businesses in St Andrews, while his brother Andrew developed this remarkable collection of early golf balls.
