This directory is designed to be a comprehensive reference for collectors, dealers, and enthusiasts of antique golf balls. Here's how to get the most from it:

üìñ Understanding Ball Entries

Each ball entry contains the following information:

Ball Name

The official name as stamped on the ball or used in period advertisements.

Ball Name Format

Description of how the name appears on the ball (e.g., "Ball name linear on 3 lines within 10 squares").

Era

Approximate production date. May be a specific year (1896) or decade (1890s).

Cover Pattern

The moulded or cut pattern on the ball's surface.

Manufacturer

The maker and often their address, as advertised in period publications.

Specifications

Weights, dimensions, and production notes.

Patents & Legal

Patent numbers, legal disputes, and trademark information.

Auction Remarks

Observed auction results, rarity notes, and collector commentary.

Value

Estimated market value in USD based on recent auction results.

Condition Grade

Where documented, the condition grade (A1-A5) from auction records.
