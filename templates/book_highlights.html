1845 marks the year in which the game of golf underwent its first major change with the invention of the gutta ball made from gutta-percha.

What is Gutta-Percha?

In Malaysia 'gueta' means gum and 'percha' means cloth. In its natural state gutta-percha is a hard and resilient substance and is derived from the sap of the Palaquium genus of tree that is indigenous to South East Asia.

To collect the gutta the Palaquium has to be felled and circular incisions made through the bark into which the gum slowly accumulates. Once the gum has been extracted, any resin is drained off to create a pure gutta substance, then is cooled and shaped into manageable cloth wrapped blocks.

The Inventors

It is still unknown who first thought of using gutta-percha to make golf balls. The three most likely candidates were all Scottish:

William Smith — A watch and clock maker from Musselburgh who started his watch-making business in 1846-1847.

Robert A. Paterson — A St. Andrews divinity student who experimented with gutta-percha during his studies.

John Paterson — Robert's half brother, who continued developing the technology after Robert left for America.

The Problem of Air Pockets

John Paterson tried to improve the technique of dispelling the small air pockets within the gutta ball that created internal cracks and made it fragile. Air trapped in the gutta caused an uneven distribution of weight that affected the balance and the ball's flying potential.

It was soon apparent that a good-quality gutta was needed to make golf balls. The early balls often broke up due to impurities in the gutta and unsophisticated production techniques.

The introduction of the gutta-percha ball democratized golf, making it accessible to the masses rather than just the wealthy who could afford expensive feather balls.

Key Milestones

  • 1845 — First gutta-percha golf balls created
  • 1850s — Hand-hammered patterns introduced
  • 1860s — Moulded mesh patterns developed
  • 1880s — Bramble pattern patented
  • 1898 — Wound rubber core ball introduced (beginning of the end for gutta-percha)
  • 1903 — End of the gutta-percha era

This directory covers the entire period from 1845 to 1903, documenting the evolution of the golf ball through 551 distinct examples from collectors and auction records worldwide.
