The manner in which ball names were stamped or moulded onto gutta-percha balls varied considerably between manufacturers. Understanding these styles helps with identification and dating.

The Premier Ball Style

Ball name in 3 lines on a large disc.

The Springvale 27 Style

Ball name on a flat disc around 7 pimples.

The W. Fernie 27 Style

Ball name within a circular panel containing a number, all on a smooth disc.

The Agrippa Style

Ball name in raised letters on a flat disc with a gap around a single pimple.

The Acme 27 Style

Ball name and DWT number in 3 lines on a large disc.

The Spalding White Red Dot Style

Ball name in raised letters around a central bramble.

The Melfort 27 Style

Ball name circular on a smooth disc containing a DWT number.

The Ocobo Style

Ball name circular on a circular raised band containing a hatched area.

The Haskins Hoylake Style

Ball name circular within a band containing the DWT number.

The Musselburgh Style

Ball name on a rectangular panel.

The Vardon Flyer Style

Ball name on a circular raised band containing 7 brambles.

These naming conventions help collectors identify authentic balls and understand the manufacturing techniques of different makers. Many of these styles are covered by patents and represent significant innovations in golf ball production.
