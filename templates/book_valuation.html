Valuing antique golf balls requires understanding multiple factors including rarity, condition, historical significance, and provenance. The values in this directory represent market estimates based on auction results and private sales.

Factors Affecting Value

1. Rarity

Balls from obscure manufacturers or with unusual patterns command premium prices. Limited production runs increase desirability.

2. Condition

An A1 ball may be worth 2-3 times more than an A3 example of the same type. Original paint is highly prized.

3. Historical Significance

Balls associated with major tournaments, famous golfers, or important patents carry additional value.

4. Provenance

Documented ownership history, especially from notable collections like the H.B. Wood Collection, adds value.

5. Manufacturer

Balls from renowned makers like Allan Robertson, Robert Forgan, or the Gutta Percha Co. are particularly sought after.

6. Pattern

Unusual cover patterns (hand-hammered, unique mould designs) attract collector interest.

Auction vs. Private Sale

Auction houses (Bonhams, Mullock Jones, Golden Age Golf Auctions) typically achieve higher prices for rare items due to competitive bidding, but charge buyer's premiums (15-25%).

Private sales may offer better value for buyers, but require knowledge of the market and trust between parties.
