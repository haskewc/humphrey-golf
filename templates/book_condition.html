Understanding condition grades is essential for collectors. The following standardized grading system is used throughout this directory for balls, wrappers, and boxes.

â›³ Balls

A1 Condition

100% paint coverage, without marks or blemishes of any size. The ultimate condition for collectors.

A2 Condition

75% PLUS paint coverage, from a high just short of A1 to one that has some very small areas of paint loss and/or with a slight hair line crack or defect.

A3 Condition

40% PLUS paint coverage on about half the surface, and/or a scruffy appearance and could have several marks, cuts or cracks.

A4 Condition

DISTRESSED with 0-40% paint coverage, a ball retrieved from a pond, serious defects to the cover, deep cuts, and/or missing areas of the ball's cover.

A5 Condition

Repainted, Refurbished and/or Decorated. These balls have been altered from their original state.

W1 Condition

MINT wrapper and label as fresh as it left the manufacturer.

W2 Condition

WELL ABOVE AVERAGE from an example that is almost complete down to one with a small tear or two.

W3 Condition

AVERAGE TO BELOW AVERAGE scruffy appearance, oxidisation, some paper lacking and/or missing a label.

W4 Condition

WELL BELOW AVERAGE TO DISTRESSED with serious damage to the paper, or only remnants of the wrapping exists and without the label.

B1 Condition

Clean and sharp, the ultimate, as it left the printers.

B2 Condition

WELL ABOVE AVERAGE from an almost complete example down to one with maybe a tear, graffiti, tape repair or other damage.

B3 Condition

AVERAGE TO BELOW AVERAGE with only remnants of card board, all scruffy with major repairs.
